# Targeting & Screening Rubric + Target-List Template

**Owner:** Workstream A (Demand-Discovery)  
**Envelope:** ~90 days, ~$6k cash, ~15 conversations  
**Fixed instrument tiers (verbatim):** (A) non-refundable paid pilot >=$10k = gold; (B) written commitment to insert a reliability metric-panel into RFP/questionnaire language = silver; (C) named budget owner with recent >$25k tool-eval spend = discovery only; verbal enthusiasm = zero.

---

## Buyer classes (verbatim)

(1) enterprise platform/security/procurement leads who have approved or blocked a coding-agent deployment; (2) agent vendors (engineering teams, private failure-discovery); (3) risk-side: AI-assurance bodies, an AI-liability carrier/MGA, a Big-4 assurance practice.

---

## Class 1 — Enterprise (platform / security / procurement)

### Qualifying criteria (must meet >=5 of 8)

1. Title or function includes platform engineering, developer experience, application security, security architecture, IT procurement, vendor management, or AI governance with direct influence on coding-agent adoption.
2. Has personally **approved or blocked** a coding-agent (or equivalent LLM coding tool) deployment in the last 24 months — verified in screening, not assumed from title.
3. Organization has production software delivery (engineering headcount typically >=50 or clear multi-team SDLC).
4. Can name a budget owner (self or other) for tool-evaluation spend.
5. Has participated in a tool evaluation with **>$25k** spend in the last 18 months (line item + tool + quarter — see verification method below).
6. Organization issues RFPs, security questionnaires, or formal vendor assessments for developer tools.
7. Can speak to outage, audit, or compliance exposure from false “done” agent completions.
8. Geographic/timezone feasible for a 30-minute call within the ~90-day envelope.

### Hard disqualifiers (any one = reject)

1. **No deployment authority or influence** — cannot approve, block, or materially recommend coding-agent deployment; pure individual-contributor curiosity with no chain to decision.
2. **Vendor-paid-badge interest** — seeks a certification badge, marketing logo, or “preferred partner” status rather than independent evaluation evidence.
3. **Will not name budget or process** — refuses to identify any budget owner and has no RFP/questionnaire path; pure “send me a deck.”
4. **Competing evaluation product seller** in the same category seeking reverse diligence without a buyer seat (unless routed as Class 2 vendor under Class 2 rules).
5. **Enthusiasm-only** — high verbal interest with zero named budget owner, zero questionnaire presence, zero path to A/B instruments. Enthusiasm is never a qualifier.

### Title / keyword sourcing lists

**Titles:** Head of Platform, VP Engineering, Director Developer Experience, CISO, Deputy CISO, AppSec Lead, Security Architect, Head of AI Governance, IT Procurement Manager, Vendor Risk Manager, CTO (mid-market), VP IT, Enterprise Architect.  
**Keywords / signals:** “coding agent,” “Copilot,” “AI pair programmer,” “developer productivity,” “vendor risk questionnaire,” “AI acceptable use,” “tooling RFP,” “SOC2 vendor,” “false completion,” “agent deployment blocked.”

## Class 2 — Agent vendors (engineering / private failure-discovery)

### Qualifying criteria (must meet >=5 of 7)

1. Ships or will ship a coding agent / autonomous coding product to external customers.
2. Engineering or product lead who owns quality, evals, reliability, or enterprise readiness (not pure marketing).
3. Has private failure modes they cannot fully disclose publicly (customer incidents, silent false done-claims).
4. Sells or plans to sell into enterprise buyers who run security/procurement questionnaires.
5. Can name who would pay for a private sealed evaluation (founder, eng lead, product, or enterprise sales budget).
6. Has or is building an enterprise motion (security review, pilot contracts, procurement path).
7. Feasible for a 30-minute technical conversation in the envelope.

### Hard disqualifiers (any one = reject)

1. **Badge-only buyer** — only wants a public pass badge / marketing cert without paying for a non-refundable run.
2. **Requests sealed-suite customization or cert-prep consulting** as a precondition (conflicts with hard rules).
3. **No engineering stakeholder** — sales-only contact who cannot discuss failure discovery or eval design.
4. **Will not discuss private failure discovery** — only wants competitive public leaderboard optics.
5. **Verbal enthusiasm with no budget owner path** — same zero rule as instrument tiers.

### Title / keyword sourcing lists

**Titles:** Founder/CEO (agent startup), VP Engineering, Head of Applied AI, Eval Lead, Reliability Eng Lead, Product Lead (Enterprise), Solutions Architect (pre-sales technical).  
**Keywords:** “coding agent,” “agent evals,” “enterprise readiness,” “hallucination rate,” “task completion,” “private benchmark,” “SOC2,” “customer pilot.”

## Class 3 — Risk-side (assurance / underwriter / Big-4)

### Qualifying criteria (must meet >=5 of 7)

1. Role at AI-assurance body, AI-liability carrier/MGA, or Big-4 / major assurance practice with AI or cyber product adjacency.
2. Can speak to evidence standards for underwriting, assurance opinions, or control testing.
3. Influence over LOI language, underwriting guidelines, or assurance methodology (not pure research intern).
4. Interest in **independent, registered** evaluation evidence (not vendor self-attestation alone).
5. Can name whether a metric panel could feed underwriting/assurance use cases.
6. Path to a non-binding LOI or formal evidence-standard input within ~90 days (or clear blocker).
7. Call-feasible in envelope.

### Hard disqualifiers (any one = reject)

1. **No underwriting/assurance use case** — pure academic interest with zero product or policy path.
2. **Requires sale of cert-bank access or suite customization** as a condition of engagement.
3. **Cannot discuss LOI or evidence standards at all** (PR-only contact).
4. **Enthusiasm-only** with no named internal owner for LOI or standards work.

### Title / keyword sourcing lists

**Titles:** Underwriter (AI/cyber), MGA product lead, Head of AI Assurance, Partner (Digital Assurance), Cyber actuarial, Tech E&O product manager, AI risk methodology lead.  
**Keywords:** “AI liability,” “model risk,” “assurance opinion,” “cyber underwriting,” “controls testing,” “independent evaluation,” “registry,” “severity-weighted.”

**Not accepted alone:** “We spend a lot on tools” / “yes, over $25k.”

**Required verification (Class 1 for instrument C; used in screening for all classes where claimed):**

| Field | Required |
|--------|----------|
| Named line item or cost center | Yes (e.g., “DX tools — cost center 4412”) |
| Named tool or vendor evaluated | Yes (e.g., GitHub Copilot enterprise, Cursor, Sourcegraph) |
| Quarter (or month+year) of spend | Yes (e.g., “2025 Q3”) |
| Approximate amount | Yes; must be >$25k for the evaluation/procurement cycle cited |
| Who can confirm | Name/title of budget owner or procurement contact |

**Pass C-discovery only if:** budget owner named **and** >$25k verification complete per above.  
**Instrument tier reminder:** (C) named budget owner with recent >$25k tool-eval spend = discovery only; verbal enthusiasm = zero.

## 5-question screening script (all classes; adapt pronouns)

Use on first live contact or qualifying call. Do not score enthusiasm.

1. **Role / class fit:** “In your current role, do you approve, block, or formally recommend coding-agent deployments — or underwrite/assure them — or ship an agent product? Which of those is closest?”  
   *Fail if none and no path to a named decision owner.*

2. **Deployment authority (enterprise) / product ownership (vendor) / evidence use (risk):** “Have you personally approved or blocked a coding-agent deployment in the last 24 months?” *(Enterprise must be able to answer yes with example. Vendor: “Do you own quality/evals for a shipped agent?” Risk: “Would independent completion-reliability evidence ever enter an underwriting or assurance decision you touch?”)*

3. **>$25k verification:** “Have you run a tool evaluation over $25k in the last 18 months? If yes: which tool, which quarter, which line item or cost center, and who owned the budget?”  
   *Record answers; do not accept unsubstantiated self-report for C.*

4. **Questionnaire / RFP presence:** “Does your org currently have an RFP, security questionnaire, or vendor assessment where coding-agent or LLM-tool reliability language could appear? Y/N and name of document if known.”

5. **Instrument path:** “If independent evidence of completion reliability were useful, would the next concrete step be (A) a paid private evaluation, (B) inserting metric-panel language into a questionnaire/RFP, or (C) introducing a named budget owner for discovery — understanding verbal interest alone counts as zero for our process?”

**Screening outcome codes:** `QUALIFY-CLASS-{1|2|3}` | `HOLD-VERIFY` | `REJECT-<disqualifier code>`.  
No enthusiasm-based qualifier anywhere.

## A/B/C/zero scoring rules

Score **only** from the fixed instrument-tier block and the evidence tests below. Do not upgrade for enthusiasm, title prestige, or promised-but-unsigned documents.

### Structural blocks (sheet / scorer must enforce)

1. **Instrument may not be `A` unless all of:**
   - `amount` is numeric and `amount >= 10000`
   - `refundability` is exactly `non-refundable`
   - `evidence_link` is not `none` (points to countersigned terms + invoice)
   - `signer` is not `n/a` (named countersigner on terms)
2. **Instrument may not be `B` unless:**
   - `evidence_link` is not `none` (attached written commitment: buyer letterhead or buyer email)
   - commitment names the questionnaire/RFP and a timeline (verified by scorer reading the attachment; if either missing → not B)
   - **B evidence must come from the org that owns the questionnaire/RFP** (the buyer). A commitment authored by a vendor or any party that does not own the questionnaire/RFP is not B (→ zero unless it independently qualifies as A/C).
3. **Instrument may not be `C` unless:**
   - `budget_owner_named` starts with `Y:` and names a person
   - >$25k tool-eval spend verified per targeting #1 method: named line item, tool, and quarter (not self-report alone); note verification in evidence or scorer notes referenced by `evidence_link` or status notes
4. **Else `zero`.** Verbal enthusiasm = zero. Partial interest = zero. Unsigned pilot discussion = zero. "We'll put something in the RFP later" without written commitment = zero.

### Classification procedure (apply in order)

| Step | Test | If true | If false |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Countersigned terms + invoice present; amount >=10000; marked non-refundable | **A** (gold) | go to 2 |
| 2 | Written commitment on buyer letterhead/email naming questionnaire and timeline | **B** (silver) | go to 3 |
| 3 | Named budget owner + >$25k tool-eval spend verified (line item, tool, quarter) | **C** (discovery only) | **zero** |

**Precedence:** A beats B beats C. A single row gets one instrument value only. A row that fails A/B structural blocks is re-evaluated at the next step (e.g., unsigned pilot with verified budget owner → C, not A).

**Instrument-tier definitions (score against these only):** (A) non-refundable paid pilot >=$10k = gold; (B) written commitment to insert a reliability metric-panel into RFP/questionnaire language = silver; (C) named budget owner with recent >$25k tool-eval spend = discovery only; verbal enthusiasm = zero.

## 1. SOP — Questionnaire / RFP Audit

**Time budget:** cold reader completes a supplied fixture in <=2 hours (obtain + scan + gap map + readout draft). Live buyer materials may take longer; do not skip steps.

### Step 0 — Preconditions
1. Conversation is logged in the instrument ledger with class, org, contact, consent.
2. Buyer has confirmed they use an RFP, security questionnaire, vendor questionnaire, or equivalent procurement instrument for coding-agent / AI-developer-tool purchases (supports dashboard “RFP/questionnaire presence Y”).
3. Operator has written consent to receive and retain the document for evaluation purposes only.
4. Operator will not offer cert-prep consulting, will not customize any sealed suite for the buyer’s preferred vendor, and will not imply payment buys a passing score (hard rules).

### Step 1 — Obtain the instrument
1. Request the **current** questionnaire or RFP section that governs coding agents, AI coding tools, LLM-assisted development, or adjacent AI tooling — not a future draft only.
2. Accept: PDF, DOCX, portal export, or email body of questions. Note version/date and owning team (security, procurement, platform, risk).
3. Record source metadata for the ledger: org, document title, version/date, section scope, contact who supplied it.
4. If the buyer has **no** such instrument: stop the audit; mark RFP/questionnaire presence = N; do not invent sections. Offer only to help them draft a first insertion later (still requires written commitment for instrument B).

### Step 2 — Keyword scan
1. Run a case-insensitive search over the full text for every term in **§2 Keyword scan list**.
2. For each hit, tag: **section ID / page**, **exact phrase (short quote)**, **bucket** (Present / Partial / Absent-as-control).
3. “Partial” = related word appears but **not** as a requirement for independent, registered evaluation of completion reliability (e.g., “hallucination” in a risk essay, or “SLA” only for uptime).
4. After automated/search scan, do a **manual pass** on: evaluation / acceptance criteria; security questionnaire AI/ML addenda; model risk / third-party risk; incident & rollback; benchmarking claims; payment/acceptance milestones.
5. Complete the scan checklist table (mark Y/N for each keyword family). Do not stop at the first reliability-adjacent word.

### Step 3 — Gap map
1. Fill **§3 Gap-map template** one row per material section that *should* host reliability controls (evaluation, acceptance, security AI addendum, third-party risk, SLAs/ops, vendor claims).
2. Columns: Section | Current language (quote or “none”) | Gap | Recommended insertion (`RFPI-1` and/or `RFPI-2` + placement note).
3. Every gap that needs new buyer language must recommend **at least one** of `RFPI-1`, `RFPI-2`. Prefer:
   - `RFPI-1` for narrative RFP / contract-style schedules and acceptance criteria.
   - `RFPI-2` for checkbox / short-answer questionnaire items.
   - Both when the buyer uses a dual packet (RFP + security questionnaire).
4. Explicitly note absence of the five-metric panel and of public-registry evidence (`PI-1 §Registry` is the evidence standard named in the insertion pack; do not redefine it here).
5. Flag any language that would conflict with hard rules if Sancho were named (e.g., buyer asks vendor to “guarantee pass,” “customize the test bank,” or “prep for certification”). Recommend neutralizing language in the readout, not a Sancho exception.

### Step 4 — Buyer readout
1. Send **§5 Buyer readout email** (adapt names; keep metric strings and pilot-fact citation format).
2. Attach or paste the completed gap map.
3. Make **one specific ask**: written commitment to insert `RFPI-1` and/or `RFPI-2` into a named questionnaire/RFP by a named date (instrument B). Do not count verbal enthusiasm.
4. Optional parallel path: if budget owner is named and spend verification exists, note instrument C is discovery only; pilot path remains instrument A (non-refundable >=$10k).

### Step 5 — Ledger attachment (instrument B path)
1. Store: source questionnaire (or redacted excerpt), completed gap map, readout email, and — when received — buyer written commitment (letterhead or email naming questionnaire + timeline).
2. Ledger scoring uses the fixed instrument-tier block only: B requires written commitment on buyer letterhead/email naming the questionnaire and timeline; else zero for B.
3. Audit alone (gap map without written commitment) is **not** instrument B; it is supporting work product. Mark evidence link to the audit pack; instrument field stays zero until commitment arrives.

### Step 6 — Quality gate before send
- [ ] Keyword scan list fully marked (no blank rows).
- [ ] Gap map has >=1 row with recommended insertion citing `RFPI-1` and/or `RFPI-2`.
- [ ] First buyer-facing mention of metrics uses full canonical strings.
- [ ] Pilot fact, if used, matches fixed citation format exactly.
- [ ] No use of the word "lie"; no detection claim beyond ~7% recall; no offer to sell/customize cert bank or cert-prep consulting.
- [ ] Readout contains a single written-commitment ask (instrument B), not a soft “let’s stay in touch.”
